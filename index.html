More Information

Hanfu

Also known as the "Han clothing", is the oldest style of Chinese traditional clothing. It can be traced back 4,000 years to the time of Huangdi. It basically consists of a yi, or a narrow-cuffed, knee length tunic tied with a sash and a narrow, ankle-length skirt, called a chang, worn with a bixi(a length of fabric) had more choices when it came to the color and style of hanfu compared to men. Its influence has reached other neighboring countries such as Korea, Japan, and Vietnam, which is why similar clothing such as the cheongsam and kimono are seen today.It was a the national clothing of the Han ethnic group.

Qipao

Known as the cheongsam, derived from the Cantonese word "cheuhngsaam" this type of clothing originated in Shanghai in the 1920s. It exploded in popularity among schoolgirls and celebrities. A representation of the "modern" woman in China, its sleek design made it into a fashion phenomenon. Its popularity was mainly among women, but a mens version of the qipao was also created.

Qing Dynasty

The Qing Dynasty was mainly ruled by the Manchurians after they invaded China from the North. So Qing Dynasty fashion often reflected the features of the Manchurian culture, such as horses.The intricately hand-sown outfits shown above were only for the ruling Emperor and the Empress Dowager. Commoners normally wore less colorful and elaborate outfits. As shown in the picture, there were prominent features in Qing Dynasty fashion. The sleeves were usually long and ended at the top of the hands in the shape of a horse hoof. Therefore, it is usually called the "horse-hoof sleeve".
